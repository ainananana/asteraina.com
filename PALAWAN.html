THE SOLAR SYSTEM

 The solar system has eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth,
Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus & Neptune. There are five officially recognized dwarf planets
in our solar system: Ceres, Pluto, Haumea, Makemake & Eris
The Solar system is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it.
It formed about 4.6 billion years ago when a dense region of a molecular cloud collapsed, forming the Sun and a protoplanetary disc
.




THE SUN

-The Sun is a yellow dwarf located at the center of our solar system.
-It is a massive, hot ball of plasma, inflated and heated by energy produced
by nuclear fusion reactions at its core.
-The Sun is by far the largest object in our solar system, containing 99.86%
of its total mass.
-The Sun is a typical star that maintains a balanced equilibrium by the fusion of hydrogen into helium at its core, releasing this energy from its outer photosphere.
Astronomers classify it as a G-type main-sequence star.








PLANETS



MERCURY

- Mercury: The smallest planet in our solar system, Mercury is also the closest to the Sun.
Its surface is heavily cratered, resembling the Moon,
and it experiences extreme temperature variations due to its lack of atmosphere.





VENUS

- Venus: Venus is the hottest planet, with a surface temperature hot enough to melt lead.
This extreme heat is due to a runaway greenhouse effect caused
by its dense atmosphere, primarily composed of carbon dioxide.




EARTH

- Earth: Our home planet, Earth is unique in our solar system for possessing
liquid water on its surface and supporting a wide variety of life.
Its atmosphere provides protection from harmful solar radiation
and regulates temperature.




MARS

- Mars: Often called the "Red Planet" due to the iron oxide prevalent on its surface,
Mars is a cold, desert world with a thin atmosphere.
Scientists are actively searching for evidence of past or present life on Mars.




JUPITER

- Jupiter: The largest planet in our solar system, Jupiter is a gas giant composed primarily of hydrogen and helium.
Its iconic Great Red Spot is a massive, long-lived storm larger than Earth.




SATURN

- Saturn: Famous for its spectacular ring system, Saturn is another gas giant with a composition similar to Jupiter.
These rings are made up of countless icy particles and dust.




URANUS

- Uranus: An ice giant, Uranus has a unique axial tilt of nearly 90 degrees, meaning it essentially rotates on its side.
This extreme tilt leads to unusual and long seasons.




NEPTUNE

- Neptune: The farthest planet from the Sun, Neptune is a dark, cold, and incredibly windy ice giant.
It was the first planet to be discovered through mathematical predictions
rather than direct observation.
